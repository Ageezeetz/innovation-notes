	- A company called Dig Energy is creating more **compact geothermal drillers** to produce energy, making geothermal heating and cooling way **cheaper**
	- Claim that using these new rigs would **decrease drilling costs by 80%** and would **replace fossil fuel burners and furnaces**
	- Heating and cooling represent about 1/3 of the US's energy use, and can even go up to 40%
	- Typical geothermal energy generation is not cheap due to it's upfront cost, as it is really expensive
	**- About 1% of homes use geothermal energy, leaving the rest (99%) to use other sources (non-renewable, etc)**
	- There are **two types of geothermal drills**, one that **drills deep into the Earth**, reaching up to temperatures of hundreds of degrees, and others that one **dig a few hundred feet into the Earth**. These superficial drills generate a **low, but CONSTANT temperature (perfect for cooling/heating),** which is what Dig Energy focuses on)
	- Typical superficial drills use underground pipes to transfer tempered water to cool/heat; costs of installing these pipes and drilling is about 30% of the total cost of the operation. Dig Energy's strategy of using water jets to drill holes instead of using normal drills, decreasing costs
	- Holes generated by water jet drill is much thinner and cleaner than typical drill's holes; also goes in a straighter line, allowing placement of multiple generators closer to each other
	- Plan on selling devices to other drillers (companies)
